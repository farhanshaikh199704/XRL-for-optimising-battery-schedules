# XRL for Optimising Battery Schedules

**Master's Thesis — Farhan Shaikh, Hertie School, 2025**

Explainable reinforcement learning for economic battery dispatch on the German EPEX Day-Ahead Market (DAM). Extends Sage et al. (ICCAD 2023) to the German market with Temporal Fusion Transformer price forecasting, IDM intraday features, and a three-question XRL explainability pipeline.

---

## Research Questions

**RQ1 — Action attribution:** Why did the agent choose action $a$ at hour $t$?
Method: DeepSHAP on the DQN Q-network (model-explaining, post-hoc, local).

**RQ2 — Temporal P&L decomposition:** Which hours in the delivery day drove daily profit and loss?
Method: Hourly reward decomposition and Q-value advantage (reward-explaining, EDGE-style critical timestep).

**RQ3 — Pipeline attribution:** For a big-loss event, was it forecast error, policy error, or both?
Method: Oracle counterfactual state — TFT forecasts replaced with realised prices to isolate which pipeline stage failed (novel extension of Saulieres 2025 §4.3).

## Dataset

**`german_epex_dam_idm_2022_2025.csv`**

35,016 hourly observations from 3 January 2022 to 31 December 2025.

| Feature group | Columns |
|---|---|
| DAM price lags | `DAM_t1` – `DAM_t5` (24h to 120h) |
| Neighbour zone | `AvgNeighbour_t1` (DE/LU average, 1h lag) |
| IDM intraday | `IDM_t1`, `IDM_t24`, `DAM_IDM_spread_t1` |
| Renewables | `Wind_offshore_t1`, `Wind_onshore_t1`, `Solar_t1` |
| Load | `Net_load_t1`, `Residual_load_t1` |
| Cyclic time | `sin_hour`, `cos_hour`, `sin_weekday`, `cos_weekday`, `sin_month`, `cos_month` |
| TFT metadata | `time_idx` (0–35,015), `group_id` (`DE_LU`) |
| Target | `pool_price` (EUR/MWh) |

Price statistics: mean 124.67 EUR/MWh, range −500 to +936.28 EUR/MWh, 1,400 negative-price hours (4.0% of dataset). TFT train/val split: 2022–2024 training, 2025 H1 validation, 2025 H2 unseen test.

## Rule-Based Demonstrations

DQN training optionally pre-fills the replay buffer with rule-based demonstration data following Sage et al. (ICCAD 2023) Algorithm 1. Enable in `run_dqn.py`:

```python
EXP_PARAMS['use_demonstrations'] = True
EXP_PARAMS['demo_breakeven'] = 47.3   # EUR/MWh
```

The rule: discharge at full power when `pool_price > 1.5 × breakeven` and `SOC > 0.3`; charge at full power when `pool_price < 0.5 × breakeven` and `SOC < 0.7`; otherwise hold. A separate demonstration buffer `Ddemo` is populated for one episode before training. During training, batches are sampled from both `Ddemo` and the agent's experience buffer `Dexp` at linearly decaying ratio `ρ = (E − e) / E`.

## XRL Explainability Pipeline

Three analysis methods grounded in Saulieres (2025) taxonomy:

**Q1 — DeepSHAP action attribution**
Taxonomy: model-explaining, post-hoc, local → global.
Output: signed SHAP bar chart per timestep; mean |SHAP| global importance ranked by feature group (TFT forecasts, price lags, renewables, load, cyclic, SoC).

**Q2 — Temporal P&L decomposition**
Taxonomy: reward-explaining, temporal, local → global.
Output: four-panel daily waterfall (hourly reward, pool price + actions, SoC trajectory, Q-value advantage); hourly suboptimality rate across all episodes.

**Q3 — Pipeline attribution**
Taxonomy: novel counterfactual, post-hoc, local.
Output: big-loss events classified as `forecast_error`, `policy_error`, `both`, or `market_event` by replacing TFT p50 forecasts with realised prices and re-running the DQN forward pass. Pie chart and scatter of forecast MAE vs loss magnitude.

## Key Design Decisions

**pool_price excluded from observation space.** The realised clearing price at hour `t` is not available to the agent when making its dispatch decision — it is only used to compute the reward after the fact. This prevents lookahead leakage. The TFT p50 serves as the agent's price expectation instead.

**DOD degradation model.** Battery ageing cost is computed per step based on the change in depth-of-discharge, following Yi Dong et al. (2021). Breakeven at 47.3 EUR/MWh means any dispatch below this price destroys value.

**Hourly resolution, 8,760-step episodes.** Longer episodes than most related work — capturing full seasonal patterns at the cost of delayed rewards. This is the core motivation for demonstration-assisted training.
